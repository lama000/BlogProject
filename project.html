Media and digital technology .. Orientation towards the Internet

Internet advertising in 2016 amounted to about $177 billion globally, which is equivalent to a third of the total ads of all types registered in the same year. Google got the largest share with about $79.4 billion, while Facebook ranked second with about 26.9 billion, and if we add to them what Baidu, Microsoft, Yahoo, Verizon and Twitter got, we find that these seven companies won three quarters of Internet advertising revenues in the world.

The value of online advertising has exceeded the value of television advertising, to become the largest advertising medium in the world, according to data and analysis by Zenith (a media marketing specialist).

With regard to the Arab countries, Internet advertisements recorded only about one billion dollars in 2016. Although this figure represents only 5% of the total Arab advertising spending for the same year, it is witnessing a significant annual growth of about 20%, compared to the decline in advertising spending on other media. This is what alerts us to the need to increase interest in digital media, and to build advertising partnerships with major international Internet companies, as it is noted, for example, that advertisements provided by Google are now appearing on respected media networks.

Media and digital technology .. continuous development and new challenges

The global media industry is going through a sharp inflection point under the influence of rapid technological developments. Most experts expect a complete end to paper journalism within a decade or two at most. Media and digital technology are constantly challenged to fit in and keep pace with audience preferences about styles and styles that are appropriate for them.

But this is not the only issue facing the media. Even the traditional media, which has turned completely digital, is facing major challenges in terms of revenue due to the dominance of major Internet companies - such as Google and Facebook - in the digital advertising market. He also faces other challenges arising from being under pressure to catch up with the rapid technological developments that are changing people's reading habits.

In the United States, many newspapers and magazines stopped publishing their paper copies long ago. The Christian Science Monitor stopped publishing its print edition in 2009, followed by the US News and World Report in 2010, and Newsweek in 2013. Many American, English and French newspapers and magazines followed suit, among others.
